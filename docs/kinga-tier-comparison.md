## Part A: Insurer Tiers

Every insurer tier runs the identical nine-stage KINGA pipeline on every claim. The per-claim fee pays for pipeline execution. The platform fee pays for the access tier. What changes between tiers is not the quality of analysis — it is what the insurer is permitted to see and act on.

## Part B: Assessor Tiers

Assessors are a separate customer type with a separate interface and a separate value proposition. Assessor outputs are technically and contractually isolated from insurer-tier intelligence. Every assessor-tier report carries a digital watermark identifying it as an assessor-produced document. Assessors may incorporate KINGA conclusions into their own narrative reports but may not share raw KINGA outputs directly with insurers.

## Part E: The Commercial Logic

The per-claim fee is the same across Tier 1, Tier 2, and Tier 3 ($12/claim) because the computation is the same. The pipeline runs once. All data is stored. The tier fee is different because the access is different.

When an insurer upgrades from Tier 1 to Tier 2, no claims are reprocessed. The data already exists. The tier gate opens and the insurer can see intelligence that was always there. This is the most important commercial property of the architecture: **upgrading is instant and costless to deliver, but immediately valuable to the insurer.**

The on-demand Forensic Intelligence Package ($100–$150 per claim) is the bridge that converts Tier 1 and Tier 2 insurers into Tier 3 prospects. An insurer who purchases five on-demand packages in a month has spent $500–$750 on top of their subscription. The upgrade to Tier 3 at $1,500/month — which includes unlimited packages plus full portfolio intelligence — is a straightforward financial decision.
